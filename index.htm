General Cat Care

Cats are amazing companions, and in order to be ready to bring one home to cuddle tonight, it's important to be aware of the effort and commitment needed to make these furbabies feel comfortable. From providing proper nutrition and regular veterinary care to creating a safe and stimulating environment, every aspect of their well-being matters. Building trust through patience and affection will help your new feline friend feel secure and loved in their forever home.

Feline Life Stages
Kitten - Birth to 6 Months
During this early developmental stage, kittens are full of energy and learning to navigate the world. These six months are crucial, as kittens experience the most life-determining changes! This is the stage where their personality begins to emerge and truly shine. Providing proper socialization and care during this time helps set the foundation for a happy, confident adult cat.
Junior - 1-2 Years Old
This playful stage of life means having an extremely active feline friend. Cats in this life stage are considered adolescents and require just as much enrichment and attention as kittens. Their energy levels remain high, and they are still learning important social and behavioral skills. Providing consistent interaction and stimulating activities will help them develop into well-adjusted adult cats.
Adult - 3-6 Years Old
These years are prime for cats, as they are considered in their prime. During this stage, cats are typically at their healthiest and most active, striking a balance between playful energy and maturity. They have settled into their personalities, making it a great time to deepen your bond with them. Regular health check-ups, a balanced diet, and mental stimulation are essential to ensure they remain happy and healthy. This is also a good time to establish consistent routines to keep your feline friend thriving.
Mature - 7-10 Years Old
During this stage, naps under the sun slowly start replacing playful activity. Don’t worry; your cat still enjoys interaction, but they may prefer calmer, more relaxed activities. It’s important to monitor their health closely, as age-related changes may begin to appear. Providing a comfortable and stress-free environment helps them adapt to their slowing pace. Regular vet check-ups, a healthy diet tailored to their age, and gentle exercise will ensure they stay happy and content during these golden years. Cherish these moments as your cat becomes an even more affectionate companion.
Senior - 11-14 Years Old
As cats enter this senior stage, they really begin slowing down. It’s common for their senses, such as vision or hearing, to decline, so they may need extra care and attention. Regular veterinary check-ups are crucial to catch and manage any age-related health issues like arthritis or kidney disease. Providing a comfortable and accessible environment with gentle bedding and easily reachable resources helps them stay at ease. Despite their age, senior cats can still enjoy gentle play and affection, making this stage of life deeply rewarding for both the cat and their owner.
Geriatric - 15+ Years Old
In the geriatric stage, cats require extra care and attention as their bodies and senses age further. They may experience significant changes, such as reduced mobility, increased sleep, or difficulty with activities they once enjoyed. Regular veterinary visits are essential to manage chronic conditions like arthritis, kidney disease, or cognitive decline. Providing a quiet and safe environment, with easily accessible food, water, and litter boxes, helps reduce stress and supports their comfort. Despite their age, geriatric cats can still enjoy love, gentle interactions, and the companionship of their humans, making their golden years special and cherished.
